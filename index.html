🇮🇳 India's own AI tech company — born from a teenager's dream

At tiny robotics, we are not just building robots — we are architecting the foundation of India's first indigenous AI technology company. Imagine a future where every Indian household has an AI companion that understands Hindi, Tamil, Bengali, and every language of this land. Where every village school has access to affordable AI tutors that adapt to each child's learning style. Where our farmers get real-time AI advice in their own dialect, and our small businesses use intelligent desktop bots to compete globally.

This is the vision that drives us. Founded by Sohan Sahu, a 16-year-old innovator who believes that age is just a number when it comes to changing the world, tiny robotics is building from the ground up — with Indian engineers, Indian components, and Indian imagination. We're not copying Silicon Valley; we're creating our own path. Our robots are being designed to understand India's diversity, to work in our conditions, and to be affordable for every Indian family.

The journey starts with tiny robots — NanoBots for kids to learn programming, DeskBots that become your productivity partners, and DIY kits that turn every young innovator into a creator. But this is just the beginning. We are laying the groundwork for an AI ecosystem that is truly Made in India, for India, and by India. From the streets of Bengaluru to the farms of Punjab, from the classrooms of Kerala to the startups of Delhi — we want every Indian to have access to intelligent machines that empower them.

We are a team of dreamers, builders, and believers. And we're just getting started. The robots will be available soon — but the revolution has already begun. Join us in building India's AI future. 🇮🇳

— Sohan Sahu, 16 · Founder, tiny robotics
